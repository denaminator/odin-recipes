Jerk-Spiced Salmon and Quinoa Bowl w/Mango Vinaigrette

Chef Millie Peartree has been feeding the community in the Bronx, where she grew up, as a private chef, caterer and restaurant owner of Millie Peartree's Fish Fry & Soul Food for more than a decade. Her healthy-eating philosophy is all about making simple but flavorful protein-packed meals. Mango juice in the vinaigrette balances the spiced salmon and roasted vegetables on these grain bowls.

Sourced from EatingWell


Yield = 6 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs skin-on salmon fillet
  • 8 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 2 tbsp + 1 tsp jerk season, divided
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 4 tsp lime juice, divided (plus lime wedges for serving)
  • 1 lb Brussel sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground pepper, divided
  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cut in 1/2" cubes
  • 1 large shallot
  • 2 cups low-sodium vegetable broth (plus more if needed)
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1/4 cup mango juice or nectar
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro
  • 3/4 cup dried cranberries, coarsely chopped

Steps

  1. Place salmon on cutting board, skin-side up, and cut into 6 portions. Using a sharp knife, make 3 1/8" deep cuts through skin of each portion.
  2. Whisk 1 tbsp oil, 2 tbsp jerk seasoning, paprika, and 1 tsp lime juice in medium bowl.
  3. Rub mixture on both sides of the salmon. Cover and refrigerate for about 20 mins.
  4. Position racks in upper and lower thirds of oven; preheat to 425Â°F.
  5. Toss Brussel sprouts, 1 tbsp oil, 1/4 teaspoon salt, and 1/8 tsp pepper on rimmed baking sheet.
  6. Toss squash, 1 tbsp oil, 1/4 tsp salt, and remaining jerk seasoning on another rimmed baking sheet.
  7. Roast vegetables until lightly browned, stirring and rotating pans between racks halfway through for about 20 mins.
  8. Meanwhile, heat 1 tbsp oil in medium saucepan over medium-high heat.
  9. Add shallot and cook, stirring until softened for about 2 mins.
  10. Add broth and bring to a boil. Add quinoa, cover and reduce heat to a low simmer.
  11. Cook until most of the liquid has been absorbed for 15-20 mins. Then remove from heat.
  12. When the vegetables are out of the oven, turn broiler to high and line a baking sheet with foil.
  13. Place the salmon skin-side up, on the prepared baking sheet. Broil until the skin is crispy and the flesh is opaque for 8-10 mins.
  14. Whisk mango juice/nectar, cilantro, remaining oil, 3 tsp lime juice, 1/4 tsp salt, and 1/8 tsp pepper in a small bowl.
  15. Serve quinoa, vegetables, and salmon topped with dressing, dried cranberries, and lime wedges.
