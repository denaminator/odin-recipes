Miso-Almond Butter Cookies

Did we lose you with the miso? Don't go! It adds savory richness that amps up the nutty almond butter and brown butter flavor...but don't just take our word for it.

Sourced from Bon Appétit


Yield = About 16 cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 10 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 cup (packed) dark brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/3 cup smooth almond butter
  • 1/4 cup miso
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup bittersweet chocolate chips or wafers
  • Flaky sea salt

Steps

  1. Place racks in upper and lower thirds of oven; preheat to 350°.
  2. Whisk flour and baking soda in a medium bowl.
  3. Melt butter in a small saucepan over medium heat, swirling occasionally, until it foams then browns for 5-6 mins.
  4. Transfer to a medium bowl and let cool 5 mins.
  5. Add brown sugar and stir until well combined, about 1 min.
  6. Add egg and continue to stir until mixture is smooth, about 1 min longer.
  7. Add almond butter, miso, and vanilla and stir until well combined.
  8. Mix in dry ingredients, scraping down sides of bowl as needed, until combined.
    9. If you aren't feeling the chocolate dip (instructions below), now is the timte to stir in chocolate chips. Let dough rest 10 mins.
  9. Scoop 2 tbsps of dough and roll into a ball (you should have 16 balls). Arrange on 2 parchment-lined baking sheets, spacing 2" apart.
  10. Make a crosshatch pattern with a fork across tops of dough (if fork sticks to dough, dip into water before continuining).
  11. Bake cookies, rotating sheets halfway through, until lightly browned and firm around the edges, 12-14 mins. Let cool.
  12. Melt chocolate in a microwave in 20-second blasts, stirring in between, until completely smooth.
  13. Dip 1 side of each cookies into chocolate and return to baking sheets.
  14. Let sit at room temp until chocolate hardens, about 2 hours. Sprinkle w/sea salt.
Note: Cookies can be made 2 days ahead. Store in an airtight container at room temp.
